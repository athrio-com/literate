A *Concept* is the metalanguage primitive of LF. Concepts declare
what something *is* — schema, invariants, relationships — without
prescribing how it is *done*. A Concept is realised by a Trope,
which carries the prose and code that satisfy the Concept's
contract.

Concepts come in two scopes (ADR-010), with identical shape and
machinery:

- **At the LF level**, a Concept ships as a TypeScript workspace
  package (`@literate/concept-<id>`) that exports a typed
  `Concept<I>` value with an Effect Schema. This is how LF
  declares its own metalanguage.
- **At the corpus level**, a consumer authors their own Concepts
  as markdown files at `corpus/concepts/<slug>.md`. This is how
  they declare the reserved vocabulary of their product
  (e.g. *Tenant*, *Workflow*, *Approval Chain*).
