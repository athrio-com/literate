# Decisions

A decision is a record of an architectural or structural choice made
during a product's development. Each decision lives as a single file
in `corpus/decisions/` with an append-only body and a mutable
`Status` line. The reasoning chain is preserved; only the record of
whether the decision is in force (`Accepted`, `Deferred`,
`Superseded by ...`) is mutated over time.

The `decisions` Concept supports subkinds. v0.1 ships one subkind —
**ADR** (Architecture Decision Record) — realising the classical
form. Future subkinds (SDR for story decisions in book projects,
PDR for product decisions) realise the same Concept with different
framing and conventions.

## Shape

A decision file carries a header (`# <SUBKIND>-NNN — title`,
`Date:`, `Status:`, `Tags:`), a `Context:` block, a `Decision:` block
(optionally with structured sub-sections), and a `Consequences:`
block. An optional `Deferred:` block scopes follow-up work.

## Mutability

- Body is append-only once Accepted. No editing of `Context`,
  `Decision`, or `Consequences`.
- `Status:` line is the sole mutable part.
- Accepted → Superseded by the later decision's number, atomically
  on the new decision's Accept.
- The folder index (`decisions.md`) is fully mutable; it is
  mechanical and reflects the folder state.

## Relationships

- Realised by the `decisions` Trope with the ADR subkind.
- Depends on `corpus` (decisions live inside it).
- Depends on `category` (tags are drawn from a closed Tag category).
