# Chapters

A chapter is a multi-session development plan. Chapters exist so
that work spanning several sessions has a stable handle for
intention and scope. Sessions reference the chapter they advance;
chapters accumulate a record of what has been planned, what has
been done, and what remains.

Unlike decisions, chapters are fully mutable. They are living
plans: revised as understanding of the work evolves, retired when
completed, re-scoped when priorities shift.

## Shape

A chapter file lives at `corpus/chapters/<handle>.md` with a
`# Chapter <handle> — <title>` header. Handles may be numbers
(`1`, `2`, `3`) or slugs (`onboarding`, `migration-to-v2`). Numbers
are stable handles; they do not imply sequencing or dependency.

## Mutability

- Body is fully mutable.
- New chapter files and material revisions pass the review gate.
- Status transition `In progress` → `Completed` is ungated when it
  flows atomically from completed work.

## Relationships

- Realised by the `chapters` Trope.
- Depends on `corpus`.
- Referenced from session logs via the `Chapter:` header line.
