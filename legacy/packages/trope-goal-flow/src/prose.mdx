# Goal-Flow Trope

The workflow Trope for drafting, gating, and retiring Goals within
a session log. Goals are session-scope specifications: they name
what the session will produce and what counts as done.

## Shape of a Goal entry

```markdown
### Goal N

**Topic:** one-line description
**Upstream:** ADRs and specs motivating this work
**Scope:** bullets of what's in (optional)
**Out of scope:** bullets of what's not (optional)
**Acceptance:** bullets defining "done" (optional but encouraged)
**Notes:** tradeoffs, constraints, decisions during work
```

On Accept, two fields are added:

```markdown
**Status:** Active
**Category:** <one of goal-category members>
```

## Steps

1. **Draft.** Write the entry into the session log's `## Goals`.
2. **Present for gate.** Gate at append; gate on material
   revisions.
3. **On Accept.** Stamp `Status: Active` and `Category`. Work may
   proceed.
4. **During work.** `Notes` may be appended without a gate.
5. **On completion.** Transition `Status: Completed` atomically.
6. **On displacement.** `Status: Superseded by Goal M` atomically
   when the new Goal is accepted.
7. **On retirement.** `Status: Abandoned` — gated.

## Upstream requirement

`Upstream` must name at least one ADR or spec. If none covers the
planned work, sub-task one of the Goal is authoring (or revising)
that upstream prose.
