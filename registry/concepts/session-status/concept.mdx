# Session Status

The closed vocabulary of `Status:` values a session log can
carry. The default spontaneous lifecycle is `Open â†’ Closed`.

Either lifecycle may end at `Abandoned` under explicit Person

  `## Plan`. The log file exists with provisional Goals copied

  occurred. The session may be revised (gated) while in this
  state.
- **`Open`** â€” work in progress. The log carries
  `Started: YYYY-MM-DDTHH:MM` and an authoritative Goals list.
- **`Closed`** â€” terminal, validated by `session-end`. The
  on-disk header line carries a parenthetical timestamp
  (`Closed (YYYY-MM-DDTHH:MM)`); the timestamp lives separately
  on the `Session` Concept's `closedAt` field. The Summary is
  written, every Goal carries a terminal status, every Plan
  entry (if any) is realised or carried forward.
- **`Abandoned`** â€” terminal, retired without completion.
  Gated. Reachable from `Planned` (the Person decided not to
  execute) or from `Open` (in-flight retirement). The on-disk
  line may carry a parenthetical rationale.

## Schema shape

```typescript
SessionStatus = 'Planned' | 'Open' | 'Closed' | 'Abandoned'
```

The Effect Schema definition is a `Schema.Literal(...)` over
the four base values. The on-disk header line may carry a
parenthetical suffix; `parseSessionStatusBase(raw)` from this
Concept's `index.ts` strips the suffix and returns the base
literal (or `null` if the value is not in the closed set).

Terminal states (`Closed`, `Abandoned`) do not transition
further.

## Mutability of the field

The `Status:` line is mutable but transitions are restricted
to the diagram above. `Planned â†’ Open` and `Open â†’ Closed`
are performed atomically by `session-start` and `session-end`
respectively. `Abandoned` requires Person Accept of an explicit
disposition note in the session log body.
