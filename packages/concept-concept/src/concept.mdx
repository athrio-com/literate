# Concept

A Concept is the metalanguage entry for a kind of thing LF
recognises. Concepts are the *interfaces* of the three-level
algebra: they declare what something *is* — its schema, invariants,
and relationships — without prescribing how it is *done*. A Concept
is realised by a Trope, which carries the prose and code that
satisfy the Concept's contract.

## Two scopes

Concepts come in two scopes; the shape and machinery are identical
(ADR-010).

- **At the LF level.** A Concept ships as a TypeScript workspace
  package (`@literate/concept-<id>`) that exports a `Concept<I>`
  value carrying an Effect Schema for its instance shape, an MDX
  prose body, and dependency references to other Concepts. This
  is how LF declares its own metalanguage (`session`, `decisions`,
  `category`, etc.).
- **At the corpus level.** A consumer authors their own Concepts
  as markdown files at `corpus/concepts/<slug>.md`. Each file is
  an authored instance of the `concept` Concept itself: a header,
  a one-paragraph definition, a `## Used in` list, and optional
  `## Not to be confused with` and `## See also` sections. This is
  how a consumer declares the reserved vocabulary of their
  product (e.g. *Tenant*, *Workflow*, *Approval Chain*).

The two scopes share the same gating discipline: new Concepts and
material revisions pass the review gate; editorial revisions are
ungated.

## Capitalisation

Concepts authored at the corpus level are capitalised in prose
(*Person*, *Protocol*, *Approval Chain*) to distinguish them from
generic English usage. A reference that does not capitalise is not
a Concept reference.

## Relationships

- Every LF-level Concept has exactly one canonical realising Trope
  at v0.1 (ADR-008). Multi-realisation is structurally permitted
  but unimplemented.
- A Concept may depend on other Concepts; LF-level dependencies are
  typed module references and are validated at compile time.
- Corpus-level Concepts cite each other via `## See also` and are
  validated only structurally in v0.1; cross-reference resolution
  is follow-up work.
- The `concept` Concept is its own root: it declares itself.

## Open questions

- Whether a Concept's instance Schema can be parametric (one
  Concept, many Schema variants chosen by consumer config) is
  deferred. Subkinds (within a Trope) are the v0.1 mechanism for
  variation under a single Concept.
